Electricity Across The World

Electricity is essential for daily life, as majority of the activities performed daily requires access to electricity. As electricity consumption across the world continues to rise, it is important to have a better understanding of which countries have the highest consumption rate, what sources were used to generate electricity and which sectors consumed the most electrical energy.

Electricity Consumption Around The World

Throughout the years, China and United States of America (USA) are the two countries with the highest elecctricity consumpiton, with over 5.56 billion and 3.90 billion of electricity consumed respectively. Russia, India, Canada and Brazil are also among countries with high electricity consumption.

Electricity Generation Sources in Malaysia

From 2000 to 2015, Natural Gas was the main source to generate electricity. However, from 2016 to 2021, Coal has become the main source for electricity generation. The main reason for this replacement might be due to Coal not only being a cheaper option as importing Natural Gas depends on international price fluctuations, but it also meets the electricity consumption demands reliably. Throughout 2000 to 2021, electricity generated through Hydro power plants has increased significantly, as Malaysia is working towards on replacing non-renewable energy sources with renewable energy sources such as, Hydro, Biofuels and Solar PV.

Electricity Generation Sources (World)

Across the world between 2000 to 2022, Coal, Natural Gas and Hydro were the most common sources used to generate electricity, with were the same as Malaysia.

From the year 2010 onwards, renewable energy sources such as Wind, Solar PV, Biofuels and Geothermal saw an significant increase in usage for generating electricity each year. The trend of switching to renewable energy might be due to several factors, with the primary one being the reduction of carbon emissions to combat air pollution, which ultimately sparked the beginning of the change.

Electricity Consumption by Sector (Malaysia)

Between 2000 and 2022, sectors in Malaysia that consumed the most amount of electricity are Industry, Commercial and Public Services and Residential.

From 2019 to 2020, the Industry and Commercial and Public Services sector experienced a drastic decline in the electricity consumed, as this is mainly due to a pandemic in 2020, which has affected the entire world. However, for the Residential sector, it has seen a significant increase as majority of the citizen stayed at home due to the restrictions set by the government.

Electricity Consumption by Sector (World)

Similarly to Malaysia, the sectors consuming the highest amnount of electricity remain the same. However, the drop-off from 2019 to 2020 was not that significant, possibly due to several factors. One such factor is the government's quick adaptation in implementing policies during the pandemic to ensure that essential services such as hospitals, data centers and infrastructure could continue operating.
